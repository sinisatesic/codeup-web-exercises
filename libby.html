Fruiting bodies

are how mycologists refer to the mushroom fruit on the mycelial web. I’ve named my practice Fruiting Bodies, inspired by the natural processes we see around us in terms of fruiting and pruning. Plants don’t judge whether the fruit is consumed, or let fallen to be replanted as a new tree, or wilts as an offering to the life force. Like any other fruiting being, there is no judgment on you for what happens with your fruit. You are the wisdom that gets to decide what is best for your lifeforce and lifestyle.

Mushrooms

are also allies that empower us to go into the darkness, their essences are an offering to support us in our own soul’s spelunking and they are unafraid of our courage. Reproductive moments in our lives can be full of darkness and light, and abortion specifically asks us to go into our deepest caverns and find the medicine hiding in there. Another reason I call upon mushroom energy for my practice, mushrooms as the fruiting bodies of a much larger web of mycelia, remind us that we as reproductive beings also deserve a deep network of deep care behind us regardless of what happens with our fruit.

Lastly,

the name fruiting is an active verb, chosen to remind us that our reproductive life is also always in motion, dispelling any myths that having an abortion limits any future chances of conception, if that’s what we end up wanting. Just like the name I’ve chosen, I show up to my clients with active opportunities to ritualize these archetypal and profound moments in our life, acknowledging that that ritual has been a necessary and common practice in navigating gigantic feeling experiences like abortion, birth, postpartum, grief and union. I believe we deserve to touch these processes and hold them while we are held in them so that our autonomy and relationship to what happens is curated by us.

About me

As an in-house abortion doula for multiple clinics in New Mexico, the Yiya Vi Kagingde Doula Project Coordinator for Tewa Women United, trainee with The UNM Doula Trainee program, and sabbat circle facilitator, I have learned many skills to show up to my clients not just in the climactic moments of your care but in the deeper preparation and ripples afterward.
